# HR Attrition Strategic Business Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition using the IBM HR dataset to identify structural drivers of employee turnover and define high-risk employee segments.

The analysis is focused purely on business insights, KPI framing, and executive-level recommendations — without predictive modeling.

The goal is to answer:

Why are employees leaving, and what organizational factors are driving attrition?

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

The organization is experiencing a 16% attrition rate.

Initial exploration shows that attrition is not evenly distributed — it is concentrated around specific employee transition phases and work conditions.

Key concern:

- Elevated attrition during the first years after promotion
- Higher turnover among employees experiencing overtime
- Strong association between low job satisfaction and exits

This indicates potential structural misalignment between expectations, workload, and employee experience.

---

## 📊 Primary KPI

Overall Attrition Rate: 16%

---

## Supporting KPIs

- Attrition by Years Since Last Promotion
- Attrition by Overtime
- Attrition by Job Level
- Job Satisfaction vs Attrition
- Monthly Income Comparison
- Years at Company Analysis

---

## 🔎 Key Analytical Findings

### 1️⃣ Post-Promotion Risk Window

Attrition increases within the first two years following a promotion.

This suggests employees may face increased responsibility without proportional structural support.

---

### 2️⃣ Overtime Exposure

Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition rates, indicating potential burnout effects.

---

### 3️⃣ Job Satisfaction Impact

Low job satisfaction strongly correlates with attrition, highlighting experience-related drivers rather than demographic ones.

---

## ⚠️ High-Risk Employee Profile

Employees most likely to leave tend to have:

- Low job satisfaction
- Overtime exposure
- Lower job level
- Recent promotion within 2 years

---

## 💡 Business Insights

1. Employee transitions represent structural risk periods.
2. Overtime acts as a leading stress indicator.
3. Satisfaction metrics are central to retention strategy.
4. Attrition is clustered around identifiable employee states.

---

## 🏢 Executive Recommendations

1. Implement structured transition support programs after promotion.
2. Monitor overtime exposure at department level.
3. Conduct targeted engagement surveys for high-risk segments.
4. Strengthen managerial coaching during role transitions.
